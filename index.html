Research Interests

    Biomechanics of injury and repair/reconstruction in musculoskeletal tissue; functional-structural relationship in orthopaedic systems; animal surgery and preclinical experimental design; custom fabrications for biomechanical testing in animal and human cadaveric models; data managing, processing, and analysis with Git, MATLAB, R, and Python; data science applications in healthcare, especially orthopaedics

 

About Me

    I am currently the Musculoskeletal Tissue Biomechanist Research Fellow, affiliated with the DoD-VA Extremity Trauma and Amputation Center of Excellence (EACE) and Oak Ridge Institute for Science and Education (ORISE), conducting research with Melvin Helgeson et al. in the Orthopaedic Biomechanics Laboratory at Walter Reed National Military Medical Center (WRNMMC).

    I received my BS in Chemistry from Wofford College in 2015 and MS in Bioengineering from Oregon State University in 2020, working with Ravi Balasubramanian and Jim Sweeney. Starting Fall 2021, I will be joining the Harold K. Dunn Orthopaedic Research Laboratory at the Univerity of Utah School of Medicine to work with Amy Lenz while pursuing my PhD in Biomedical Engineering. My research will examine the kinematics and biomechanics of the foot and ankle after tibiotalar arthrodesis and total ankle replacement. This will include (1) using robotics to simulate human foot and ankle motion driven by in vivo motion capture and biplane fluoroscopy data and (2) performing computational statistical shape modeling of the foot and ankle to evaluate morphology influencing ankle kinematics.
